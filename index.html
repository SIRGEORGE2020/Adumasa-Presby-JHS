    CONSTITUTION

    PREAMBLE :

    We, the members of THE 2007/2008 YEAR GROUP OF ADUMASA PRESBY JHS, to fully realize our purpose, do hereby adopt and establish this constitution as the guiding instrument of our Association.

    ARTICLE 1

    Name of Association The name of the Association shall be “2007/2008 YEAR GROUP OF ADUMASA PRESBY JHS” hereafter referred to as THE GROUP 07/08.

    ARTICLE 2

    Mission and Objective
    Section 1
    Mission Statement
    The mission of THE GROUP 07/08 is to provide support to our former school and also to seek the welfare of the members. To connect with each other to help promote, and develop the life of each other.

    Section 2

    Objetive(s)
    The objective of the association shall be:
  • to help bring all the members who attended or completed Adumasa Presby JHS during the 2007/2008 year group under one umbrella to foster unity and cohesion.
  • to help provide information and assistance to all members in times of need.
  • to connect and find ways to help our alma mater (Adumasa Presby JHS).

    • ARTICLE III

    Membership
    Section 1.Membership in the THE GROUP 07/08 shall be limited to all persons who attended or completed Adumasa Presby JHS in the said year.

    Section 2.Membership in the THE GROUP 07/08 shall not be limited to persons based on their race, color, religion, gender, national origin, age, ability, gender expression, gender identity or sexual orientation.

    Section 3. Active membership in the THE GROUP 07/08 shall be conferred upon those members who, in addition to the above requirements, have paid the monthly dues in full, attend meetings regularly and adheres to the groups rules and regulations.

    ARTICLE IV

    Officers
    Section 1. The officers of the Association shall be elected from the active members and must be of good behavior.

    Section 2.

    The elected officers of the Association shall consist of leadership roles, for example – President, Vice President, Secretary, Public Relations Officer, Assistant Secretary, Organizer(s) and Treasurer, which shall serve as the Executive Committee. Other positions should be by appointments.

    Section 3.

    An elected officer that wishes to resign must give tangible reason in writing to the executive board and copy the executive committee. An official resignation letter should be submitted at least 3 months before resigning.

    Section 4. Vetting

    All members seeking to serve as officers of the Association must undergo a vetting process. Membership of the vetting committee shall comprise all the sitting executive committee members and chaired by the vice president.

    Section 5. Elections:

    A. The President in consultation with the executive committee shall appoint a committee to oversee electoral processes.

    B. An existing executive who seeks to contest for another term needs to resign a month before the next electioneering season.

    C. Interested members seeking for leadership positions must pick an application form.

    D. All officers shall be elected during the annual meeting and shall serve one-year term.

    E. All officers shall be elected by a majority of the votes cast. Only active and members in good standing are eligible to vote. If no candidate for an office receives a majority of the votes cast, then only the top two candidates will be placed on a second ballot for that office until a majority is reached.

    F. A quorum of two-thirds of the active membership shall be required for all meetings where officer nominations or elections take place.

    G. All elections shall be conducted by secret ballot.

    Section 6. Officers’ Duties:

    The President

    The President shall among other things;

  • Prepare and preside over all meetings.
  • Facilitate Executive Board meetings
  • Lead the setting and accomplishing of goals
  • Assess and fulfill the purpose and needs of the group.
  • Assist all executive officers
  • Be a signatory to the groups account
  • Coordinate all programmes
  • Coordinate the Executive Board transition
  • Create a shared vision with the group and enable others to articulate the vision
  • Be open to all opinions and input

    The Vice President
  • Preside at all Association meetings in the President's absence, and in the event of her/his resignation, shall become the President of the group for the duration of the term.
  • Direct constitutional updating and revision
  • Submit term reports
  • Serve as liaison to committees
  • Perform other duties as determined by the President.

    The Secretary

  • Prepare and maintain minutes of all Executive Board and group meetings.
  • Send information to all appropriate members.
  • Prepare an agenda with the President for all meetings
  • Prepare and share records of meeting with members
  • Maintain attendance (roll call) at all meetings
  • Maintain a calendar of events
  • Maintain a phone and e-mail directory of all members
  • Inform members of upcoming meetings and events
  • Oversee the Association’s public relations and advertising of meetings and events
  • Maintain the office and any supplies; work with the treasurer when new supplies need to be purchased.
  • In the absence of both the President and Veep, must act as the President

    The Treasurer

  • Prepare, present and defend the groups budget
  • Prepare money request/deposit forms and submit according to laid down procedures
  • Maintain financial records in an organized manner
  • Serve as chair of the Finance Committee
  • Make information available for auditing by the audit commitee
  • Make regular reports to the Executive Board and the Association
  • Coordinate solicitations and fundraisers
  • Maintain an inventory of all equipment and its condition

    The Orgainizer

  • Must ensure that venue for meetings are in place.
  • Must ensure orderliners at meetings
  • Must ensure smooth movement of members when there is a function
  • Must serve as programmes coordinator.

    Public Relation Officer (PRO)

  • To respond to unfair and unbalanced news stories pertaining to the group.
  • To make the group known throughout the community by informing the public about meetings and activities
  • To make a continuing effort to market the association to the media and the outside world
  • The Guidance and Counseling Coordinator
  • Shall be nominated by the Executive Committee and approved by a majority vote of a quorum of active members. The Coordinator must be selected from a poll of individuals put together by the Executive committee.

    Duties

  • Meet at least ones a month with the Association’s President and/or Executive Board
  • Attend Association meetings and events when possible
  • Give a report during the Association’s regular meeting
  • Keep the Executive Board informed on all matters
  • Understand and help solve the individual needs of members.
  • Encourage and assist in the setting of Association’s goals
  • Help the officers assess and fulfill their duties
  • Develop the effectiveness of the officers and members through coordinating leadership development/team-building opportunities
  • Advice ineffective leadership, negative behaviors or lack of follow through
  • Assist Treasurer with budgeting and financial issues
  • Assist with election concerns
  • Encourage annual celebrations and recognitions

    ARTICLE V

    Dues and Finances

  • Section 1 The dues for the group will be established by the Executive Committee IN consultation with members. Payment of dues must be received by the Treasurer within by the 5th day of every month.
  • Section 2 The Association will finance its activities through membership dues and fundraising projects.
